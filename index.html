MODULE 01

The Ideal Seller System

Before you walk in the door, understand the game being played against you — and why closing early is the only move.

Watch Out

What Every Seller Does to You

Sellers aren't fully honest — and if you're unprepared, you lose. They will:

  • Lie — "I'm in no hurry. The house is in perfect condition. I don't need to sell."
  • Steal — Take 3–5 hours of your time and market knowledge, then disappear.
  • Mislead — "I like your offer. I just need to think about it / sleep on it / talk to my wife."
  • Hide — Ghost you. Call from a new number? Still ignored.
Key Insight
  • This isn't malicious — it's the universal sales defense every human uses. We all say "just looking" at Best Buy. Your job is to know the game before they play it.
The System

Old Process vs. The Disruptor System

❌ What Most People Do

Qualify → Talk at them → Go for the close

Educate them → They take it elsewhere

Close at the end = maximum pressure

"What do you think?" → "I need to think about it"

✓ The Disruptor System

Close in the first 5–15 minutes (yes or no)

Disqualify throughout the rest

Signatures at the end = natural conclusion, zero pressure

The 5 Steps

The Appointment Flow

1
Establish Rules

Set the frame in the first 5–15 min. Yes or no — no thinking allowed.

2
Extract Pain

Pull the real emotional pain. Have them relive it. People buy emotionally.

3
Pull Price, Decision Makers & Time Frame

Never give your number first. Get every objection on the table before price.

4
Confirm Terms

Recap everything. Put it in writing. No new information introduced.

5
Prevent Remorse

Build a moat before you leave. Kill seller's remorse before it kills the deal.

Why "Think About It" Kills the Deal

When you let them go home, emotions cool — like walking away from a campfire. What was warm becomes an intellectual conversation with no emotional reason to sign. Then their reticular activating system kicks in: they see every competing investor's postcard, ad, and text. You just funded your own competition.

"When you hear 'I need to think about it,' hear: 'I'm not sold. You missed something.' Walking away means you're not getting that contract later."

Steven Trang
MODULE 02

Establishing Rules

Set the frame or the homeowner sets it for you. You're the professional — you call the meeting.

The Expectations Gap

Homeowner's Agenda

"Walk the house, give me a number, I'll call you if I like it."

Your Agenda

Signed contract. Today.

They sell one house per decade. You're buying several per month. You're the professional. You set the rules.

Full Script

The Framework — Word for Word

Step 1 — Open "Thank you for inviting me into your home. Can I share with you how these appointments normally go?"
Step 2 — Set Time Expectation "These meetings generally go about an hour, hour and a half. Is that still a good amount of time for you?" → If they only budgeted 15 minutes, they were never serious. Now you know.
Step 3 — Preview Their Questions "Typically, homeowners want to know three things: How does this process work? How much will I get? When do I get the money? Am I missing anything?"
Step 4 — Ask Permission for Personal Questions "For me to figure out if this is one of the houses we're going to buy, I'm going to ask questions — some may be personal. Will that be okay?" → Get permission now and they'll honor it when you need it later.
Step 5 — Set the Clear Outcome "At the end of our time together, one of two things will happen: either we work together or we won't — either way is okay. If the price doesn't work or you don't feel I'm the right fit, would you feel comfortable telling me no? Likewise, if this isn't a house I'm going to buy, will you be okay if I tell you that? And if it does make sense, we'll put everything in writing. Is that fair?"
Step 6 — Kill "Think About It" Preemptively "Can I share with you my biggest fear? You seem really nice. Some homeowners were afraid to hurt our feelings and told us they needed to think about it instead of just saying no. Can you promise me you won't do that? I promise you can't hurt my feelings. And if I hear anything besides a yes or a no, I'm going to take that as a no. Is that fair?"
Lock It In
  • After the framework: "Can you share with me what you heard?" You want genuine commitment, not a counterfeit yes. If they can repeat the rules back, they're locked in.

Positioning & Scarcity Tactics

🚪 End the Meeting to Start It
When guard is high, push them away: "Is the meeting over? Should I leave?" Once they say yes — "Now that it's over, what are you going to do?" Guard drops. Restart from zero.
💰 Scarcity Script
"You can think about it, but I only have money to buy two more houses this week. If you call me in a few days, I might be out of money."
🏠 I Came to Buy a House
If they ask what it's worth: "Sounds like you're trying to find out your home's value — I came here to buy. I've got appraiser buddies. Want me to have them call you?"
📋 Offer Collector Move
"Why don't you make me an offer?" → "I'm not in the offer-making business. I'm in the house-buying business. Sounds like you're not ready to sell."

DISC Adjustments for Rules-Setting

  • D — Driver: Say 30–45 min, not 90. Get straight to it. Skip the warmup.
  • I — Influencer: Add more small talk. Lean into the relationship. They need to like you first.
  • S — Steady: "Could you ask me enough questions to feel comfortable you're working with the right person?" — put the pace in their court.
  • C — Analytical: Walk through both scenarios explicitly. Close the future: "When I call you in 48 hours, what's your answer?"
MODULE 03

Extracting Truth, Meaning & Intent

Your job is to seek the truth — not share information. 70% them, 30% you.

The Core Principle

"To understand what Johnny buys, see the world through Johnny's eyes. You're a genius in your world, but an idiot in theirs."

Steven Trang

The brain runs at 20 million bits/sec; we communicate at 7 bits/sec. You have to be intentional about extracting what they're actually trying to tell you.

Whoever does most of the talking is doing the buying.

Core Extraction Techniques

Assume Out Loud

Make a statement instead of asking a question. People will correct wrong assumptions — but they can lie to questions. "I already know you're having a boy" → "No, we're having a girl." Wrong = corrected. Right = confirmed. Either way, you learn the truth.

Sad Puppy

People want to help someone struggling. Play uncertain. Write things down slowly. They'll jump in to rescue you. "I'm so sorry — I didn't go to law school. I'm just a real estate agent."

Never Answer an Unasked Question

"We're also talking to a realtor." — They didn't say they're going with one. Don't battle competition that isn't there. Ask: "You're telling me that because…?"

Reverse Every Question — Softened
❌ Combative

"How long have you been in business?" → "Why are you asking me that?"

✓ Softened Reversal

"That's an interesting question — what brings that up right now?"

Power Words — Use After Any Statement They Make

"And…"
Pulls more out without a question mark.
"Like…"
Invites them to give you an example or analogy.
"Because…"
Forces them to justify — reveals the real reason.
"I'm confused"
Gold when someone is lying. They have to explain themselves without you calling them out.
"Share what's on your mind"
Softest open-ended prompt. Hard to deflect.
"What do you see yourself doing?"
Surfaces their plan without asking them to commit to it.
Don't Kill Good Questions with Yes/No Modifiers
❌ Kills Intel

"Sounds like getting a guaranteed offer is important, isn't it?"

✓ Opens Conversation

"Sounds like getting a guaranteed offer is important."

Mirroring — Build Rapport Without Thinking

Speech Rate
Match their speed. Too fast = shifty. Too slow = incompetent.
Volume
Loud people need loud energy back. Quiet people need quiet presence.
Vocabulary
Match their language level exactly. Don't be smarter or simpler.
Body Language
Arms crossed? Cross yours. Leaning forward? Lean forward.

NLP — Speak Their Internal Language

Visual
Uses: looks, sees, appears, picture, clear. Match: "It seems like you're frustrated." "I can see why that matters."
Audio
Uses: sounds, heard, rings, resonates. Match: "It sounds like you're under pressure." "Does that ring true?"
Kinesthetic
Uses: feels, grip, handle, tension. Match: "It feels like you're uncomfortable." "You're trying to get a handle on this."

Playing Hard to Get

Pursued prospects run. Stay just a hair more negative than them — create some uncertainty.

Taking It Away"I don't know if we can help you." / "I'm not sure you'd even want this in writing." / "Our offer probably won't be the best — knowing that, should I even stay?"

Trap Questions — Assume the Best Case

After They Went Dark (No Response) "Hey, just calling to congratulate you on your sale — when are you guys closing?" → "We haven't signed anything yet." → "Oh, I'm confused — we assumed since you'd met with everyone and hadn't called us back, you must have sold it." → Now they tell you exactly what's wrong with the other offers.
They Already Have an Offer "Oh great! I'm sure it's an amazing offer — you should probably just sign that one. When are you guys closing?" → "Well, I haven't signed it yet." → "I don't understand. Why are you talking to me?" → Now you know what's wrong with it.

The Subwoofer — Only 3 Things Get Through Their Filter

✓ Questions They Answer
Their own words are now on the table. Harder to unsay.
✓ Assumptive Statements They Correct
Correction = truth. They'll always correct a wrong assumption.
✓ Third-Party Stories They Relate To
Fires the emotional brain. They see themselves in the story.
What Gets Blocked
  • Explaining, educating, pitching features — all filtered out
  • Anything that sounds like a presentation or sales pitch

Third-Party Story Templates

They Want to List with a Realtor"Oh, that's great — you've got options. I was talking to a family that went that route. 6 months later, hasn't sold. Realtor promised $300K — now listed at $270K. Not saying that happens to you. But if it did — how would that affect things?"
They Want the Highest Offer"Totally get that. I was talking to a family just like yours — went with the highest offer. Two days before closing, packed in their truck, ready for the next chapter — investor canceled. Not saying it happens to everyone. But if it happened to you, how would that affect things?"
They Want to Fix It Themselves"That's great — you've got the time, money, and resources. I was talking to someone who thought the same thing. Estimated $20K — opened the walls and it was $40K. Two weeks turned into 6 months. Not saying that's you. But if it happened, how would that affect your situation?"

Stop Saying These Things Immediately

  • "Is now a good time?"
  • "How are you doing today?"
  • "Are you looking for a cash offer?"
  • "Good question" (flags you as a salesperson)
  • "I understand" without explaining what you understand
  • "Just following up / touching base / checking in"
  • ARV, Retail, Terms (homeowners don't speak your language)
MODULE 04

Extracting Pain

Move them from green to red — like a doctor who diagnoses before prescribing.

Surface Pain vs. Real Pain

A foreclosure notice is a pain symptom. Real pain is: fights with the spouse, maxed credit cards, kids without stability, can't sleep, car about to be repossessed. Extract the bullet — don't just acknowledge the wound.

"Professionals sift and sort. Amateurs convince. You can't sell your way to someone who doesn't need you — but when they do, this is how you close."

Steven Trang

The Doctor Principle
  • A doctor who hears "back pain" and says "come back in 30 min for surgery" has prescribed without diagnosing. If you give a price before understanding their situation, you're doing the same thing.

Diagnostic Question Flow — Run It 2–3 Times

Full Loop "Can you tell me more about this?" → "When's the last time that happened?" → "How long has this been going on?" → "What have you tried to do about it?" → "Did that work?" → "What else have you tried?" → "Who else knows about this?" → "How do you feel about it?" → "Have you given up on fixing this?" → "What happens if we can't help you?"

Then go back and drill into each thing they mentioned. They mentioned trying to get another job? "Tell me more — how did those interviews go?" Run the loop again.

Future-Pacing — Go Shopping With Their Money

Future-Pace Script"Let's pretend 30 days from now we've bought your house and the money is in your bank account. What's the first thing you're doing with it?"
Emotional Destination"Let's say we agree today and everything's taken care of. Tonight you lay your head on the pillow knowing this is resolved. How does that make you feel?"

How You Know You've Arrived + When to Stop

Listen for: frustrated, anxious, worried, angry, tired, pissed off, exhausted, overwhelmed. Once you hear these — you're there. Don't push further or they'll break down. That's not the goal.

When They Won't Open Up
  • "We've been talking for about 30 minutes and I haven't heard one compelling reason why we should work together. What am I missing?" — If they still won't open up, this may not be a real seller. Time to leave.
MODULE 05

The Money Dance

Never give your number first. Make them negotiate against themselves.

Secure the Perimeter Before Price

Summarize & Validate"As I understand it, you're frustrated with the bank, anxious about the HOA, and you're just tired — you want this over with. Did I get that right?"
Motivation Scale"On a scale of 1–10, where 1 is 'no big deal if nothing changes' and 10 is 'we need to resolve this now' — where are you?"→ If you don't hear a 9 or 10, pump the brakes. You missed something.
Identify the Blocking Objection"I've found it's usually one of three things that stops us: timing, the price we offer, or other people involved. Which is most likely to stop us from working together?"

Pulling Their Number Out of Them

🗣️ "History Tells Me…"
"History tells me that the night before or morning of, husbands and wives usually have a conversation about what they'd feel comfortable walking away with. Would you mind sharing what you discussed?" If they resist: "Off the record, in round numbers…"
🎯 Anchor with Competitor Low
"Before I came over, cash buyers in this area are offering between $80K–$90K. I'm not saying that's my offer — but if someone offered you that, what would you do?" → They reject it → "What were you hoping to hear?" → You have their number.
🚗 The Car on the Lot
"When you bought that car — did you test drive every car in town, or only the ones with prices? We can only buy houses that have a price tag on them."
Once You Have Their Number — Close the Future First"If I could pay you that, what would happen next?"→ If they say they'd sign: great. If they mention other appointments or people: handle those first. Don't negotiate price until all objections are cleared.

Negotiation Tactics

Negotiate from Mortgage, Not Zillow
Figure out what they owe and need to walk away. If your MAO is above that — you're in business.
Split the Difference — the Right Way
Don't propose it — imply it: "It'd be a shame if we couldn't close a $10K gap…" They propose splitting. Then split again.
Closing Costs as First Throwaway
Save this as your very first concession. They feel like they won something you were already willing to give.
Last Mile + Odd Number
Moving truck. Storage unit. Rent-free month. End with an odd number ($93,713) — they think they squeezed every last dollar.
The Golden Rule
  • Never share your number until they're ready to sign. Give your offer too early → remorse or they're just collecting offers. Don't leave the price step until they've verbally committed to a number you'll pay.

Multiple Offer Situations

When They Have Multiple Offers "Boy, sounds like getting the highest offer is the most important thing to you." → "That's fair — honestly, I've never been the highest offer. Knowing that, should I even stay?" → They say stay → Isolate to one offer → "Out of all those offers, there has to be one you like most — tell me about it." → Drill into what it's missing.
MODULE 06

Decision Makers & Time Frame

First rule: never say "decision makers." Replace every instance with an indirect question.

Never Use the Word "Decision"

Every sales industry uses it. It immediately signals salesperson.

❌ Stop Asking

"Who are the decision makers?"

"Will all decision makers be present?"

"Can you make a decision today?"

✓ Ask Instead

"Who else has input on the sale?"

"Who has veto power over this?"

"Who would oppose the sale?"

"Who's going to be impacted when you sell?"

When They Say "I'm the Decision Maker""You mean to tell me if we agree on price today, you don't even need to call your wife?" → "Well, of course I'd call her." → "Perfect. Then let's get her on the phone right now."

Time Frame — Go Long, Let Them Correct You

Assume Maximum Time"I imagine you're looking to have this done by, what — Christmas?" → "Oh no, it has to be much sooner than that." → "Oh! How come it has to be done so fast?"

When they reveal urgency, they're now justifying to themselves why they need to take less for a faster close. They concluded it themselves — you said nothing about discounting for speed.

MODULE 07

Confirming Terms — The Natural Close

A close is just the natural conclusion to a well-run appointment. No pressure required.

The Golden Rule: No New Information

If you haven't talked about it, you cannot introduce it now. People have lost signed deals by mentioning how complicated it is to buy a new house — the seller said "that sounds hard, I'll just stay here."

Never Introduce at Closing
  • Your partner walking through the house
  • The escrow process or hard money details
  • How difficult it is to buy a replacement home
  • Anything not already discussed

The Closing Sequence

1
Reestablish the Rules

"Remember at the start — if the price didn't work or you weren't convinced I was your guy, you'd just tell me no. And if it was a yes, we'd put it in writing. Are we still on the same page?"

2
Ask What They Want — Don't Push

"What would you like me to do?" / "What would happen next?" — Never: "Are you ready to sign?" One is neutral. The other is pressure.

3
Guide Gently If They're Stuck

"Normally at this point, homeowners ask us to put everything in writing." → "Oh yeah, that's what I want." → "Only if you're comfortable."

4
Check the Two Key Words

"Do you feel like I understand your situation?" and "Do you believe I can help you?" — People meet you on hope, but only work with you based on belief.

Objection Annihilator — When They Still Say "Think About It"

Sad Puppy + Wrong Assumption "I'm confused — you promised me you weren't going to say that. Did I do something wrong?"
[No.] "Is it the process?"
[No.] "Oh my gosh, is it the price?"
[No.] "I get it. It's me. You're not comfortable with me." → If you've done any decent work, they jump out of their seat and tell you exactly what's holding them back. Hidden objections surface here.
MODULE 08

Preventing Seller's Remorse

Build the moat before you leave — or lose the deal from their couch.

Why Deals Fall Apart After Signing

The moment you drive away, competing investors are texting, calling, mailing, and running ads at your seller. Adult kids call: "Mom, you got taken advantage of." Two days before close they go dark. Inoculate them before you leave.

The Moat — 4 Steps Before You Leave

1
Hand Back the Contract — Ask What It Means

Hand it to them and ask: "What does this mean to you?" They'll say "It means I'm finally taking care of [the problem]." They've anchored meaning to the contract themselves.

2
Give Them Permission to Cancel — Right Now

"If there's any reason at all you feel uncomfortable, please cancel the contract now." → They refuse. By giving them the exit now, they won't take it later. And if there IS a concern, better to find out now than after they go dark.

3
Identify Potential Interlopers

"Is there anyone who might push back on this?" → Realtor friend? Adult kids? Spouse? Get it all on the table.

4
Rehearse Their Defense

"What are you going to tell them?" → If they say "It's my house, I'll do what I want" — they've committed to that position publicly. They'll hold to it.

The Moat Principle
  • You worked hard to close this deal. Build a protective moat before you leave. Handle objections that haven't happened yet — because they will, the moment you drive away.
MODULE 09

DISC Profiles

Mirror their behavior — it's the most powerful shortcut in the entire system.

The Four Profiles

D
Driver — Dominant

Traits: Direct, fast, results-only. Hates small talk. Decides instantly.

Adjust: Say 30–45 min, not 90. Get to it fast. No warmup. Project confidence.

Spot them: Cuts you off, wants the bottom line before you finish.

I
Influencer — Social

Traits: Warm, relationship-first, loves recognition and connection.

Adjust: More small talk. Be enthusiastic. They need to like you first.

Spot them: Long personal stories, lots of laughter, name-drops.

S
Steady — Supportive

Traits: Doesn't like big quick decisions. Loyal. Needs to feel safe.

Adjust: "Could you ask me enough questions to feel comfortable you're working with the right person?" Put the pace in their court.

Spot them: "I don't like making big decisions in one sitting."

C
Analytical — Conscientious

Traits: Data-driven. Needs proof. Systematic. Slow and deliberate.

Adjust: Let them think — but close the future: "When I call in 48 hours, what's your answer?" Walk through both scenarios explicitly.

Spot them: Asks for documents, slow to respond, wants every detail.

The Christmas Tree Test

  • D: Doesn't have one — or someone else put it up while they were working.
  • I: Biggest tree possible, most ornaments, multicolor chaos, maximum festive energy.
  • S: Every ornament has a story — 20 years of family memories on every branch.
  • C: Perfectly symmetrical. Lights precisely spaced. No gaps. Identical every year.

The Ultimate Shortcut

Still confused on profiles? Just mirror. Match their behavior naturally and you'll automatically build rapport — without needing to diagnose anything.

Mirroring is the most effective shortcut in the entire system. Master it and you handle any profile instinctively.

CHEAT SHEET

Full System Flow

The Disruptor's Selling System from first handshake to signed contract.

The 7-Step Appointment

1
Establish Rules (first 5–15 min)

Thank them → preview the meeting → confirm time → ask permission for personal questions → set yes/no outcome → kill "think about it" preemptively → get them to repeat it back.

2
Extract Pain (2–3 loops)

Open with "Why am I here?" → run PRIHFF panometer → revisit different angles → future-pace to spending the money → wait for emotional language.

3
Secure the Perimeter

Summarize pain → 1–10 scale → identify which of the 3 objections (timing / price / people) is most likely to stop the deal.

4
Pull Price

"History tells me…" → anchor with low competitor number → "If I could pay you that, what happens next?" → never give your number until they're ready to sign.

5
Decision Makers & Time Frame

No "decision" language → "Who has veto power?" → assume maximum time, let them correct you.

6
Confirm Terms

No new info → reestablish original rules → ask what they want (never push) → check "feel understood" + "believe you can help."

7
Prevent Remorse (before you leave)

Hand back contract → "What does this mean to you?" → offer to cancel now → identify interlopers → rehearse their defense → walk out the door.

"This is not something you learn once and master. I went through this training myself — 5 hours a week for over a year. Listen, rinse, repeat. Practice on low-stakes situations: friends, family, customer service calls. After every appointment: What went well? What didn't? How was I responsible for that outcome?"
